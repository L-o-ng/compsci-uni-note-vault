#definition 

Given vectors $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$, we define $$\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}=\Vert\mathbf{a}\Vert_{2}\Vert\mathbf{b}\Vert_{2}\cos \theta$$Where $\theta$ is the angle between $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.
It is also called the inner/scalar product.
## Properties
1) It can find the projection of a vector in the direction of another: $$\hat{\mathbf{a}}\cdot \hat{\mathbf{b}}=\hat{\mathbf{b}}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{a}}=\cos \theta$$
2) $$(s\mathbf{a})\cdot \mathbf{b}=s(\mathbf{a}\cdot \mathbf{b})$$
3) $$(\mathbf{a}_{1}+\mathbf{a}_{2})\cdot \mathbf{b}=\mathbf{a}_{1}\cdot \mathbf{b}+\mathbf{a}_{2}\cdot \mathbf{b}$$
4) $\mathbf{a}\cdot \mathbf{b}=0$ if and only if they are orthogonal or one is zero: $$\mathbf{a}\cdot \mathbf{a}=\Vert\mathbf{a}\Vert_{2}^2$$
## Calculating the Dot Product
If $\mathbf{a}=(a_{1},\dots,a_{n})$ and $\mathbf{b}=(b_{1},\dots,b_{n})$, then: $$\mathbf{a}\cdot \mathbf{b}=\sum_{i=1}^na_{i}b_{i}$$
