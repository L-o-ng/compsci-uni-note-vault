Let $f(x)$ be a function defined on an interval $[a,b]$.
A point $x_{0}\in[a,b]$ is:
1) An absolute maximum if $f(x_{0})\ge f(x)\forall x \in[a,b]$
2) An absolute minimum if $f(x_{0})\le f(x)\forall x \in[a,b]$
3) A local maximum if $\exists \updelta > 0:f(x_{0})\ge f(x_{0}+h)\forall |h|<\updelta$
4) A local minimum if $\exists \updelta > 0:f(x_{0})\le f(x_{0}+h)\forall |h|<\updelta$
Furthermore, 
5) If $f^\prime(x)=0$ and $f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x) = 0$ we **may** have a stationary point of inflection.
6) If $f^\prime (x) \ne 0$ and $f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x) = 0$ we **may** have a non-stationary point of inflection.
## Why **May**?
Consider $f(x)=x^4$.
At $x=0$, we have $f^\prime(x)=f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x)=0$.
## In General
You need to look at the **first non-zero derivative**.
+ If it is the $k^{\text{th}}$ derivative and $k$ is odd, it is an inflection.
+ If $k$ is even, it is an extremum.
