Although a [[Discrete Bivariate Distributions|Bivariate Distribution]] describes the joint probability, we may still need to explore $X,Y$ separately. This is done through **marginal probability distributions**.
>Let $X,Y$ be 2 discrete random variables taking values in the range $R,S$ respectively, with JPMF $f_{XY}(x,y)$. Then, the corresponding marginal PMFs are given by: $$\small\begin{matrix}f_{X}(x)=P(X=x)=\sum_{y \in S}P(X=x,Y=y)=\sum_{y \in S}f_{XY}(x,y) \\ f_{Y}(y)=P(Y=y)=\sum_{x \in R}P(X=x,Y=y)=\sum_{x \in R}f_{XY}(x,y) \end{matrix}$$
