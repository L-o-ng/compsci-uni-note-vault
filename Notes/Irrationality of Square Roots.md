## $\sqrt{ 2 }\notin \mathbb{Q}$

1) **Assume** $\sqrt{ 2 }\in \mathbb{Q}$ - so $\sqrt{ 2 }=\frac{m}{n}$ where $\frac{m}{n}$ is a fraction in its simplest form.
2) Then $\frac{m^2}{n^2}=2$, so $m^2=2n^2$ so $m$ is even.
3) Let $m=2k$. Then $4k^2=2n^2$, so $2k^2=n^2$ so $n$ is even.
4) Hence $\frac{m}{n}$ is not in its simplest form. This contradicts the assumption.
5) Since this contradiction followed logically from our assumption, it follows that the assumption is incorrect, so $\sqrt{ 2 }\not\in \mathbb{Q}$
