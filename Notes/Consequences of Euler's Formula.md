+ $|e^{i\theta}|=1$ since $e^{i\theta}=\underbrace{1}_{e^{i\theta}}(\underbrace{\cos \theta+i\sin \theta}_{\text{Polar representation}})$
+ $\arg(e^{i\theta})=\theta$
+ $\Re(e^{i\theta})=\cos \theta$
+ $\Im(e^{i\theta})=\sin \theta$
## The Unit Circle
![[e^itheta on the unit circle.png]]
So, 
+ $e^{i0}=1$ as proved in [[Euler's Formula]]
+ $e^{i \frac{\pi}{2}}=i$
+ $e^{i\pi}=-1$: [[Euler's Identity]]
+ $e^{\frac{3i\pi}{2}}=-i$
### Periodicity
$$e^{i(\theta+2\pi)}=e^{i\theta}$$
Just like trigonometric formulae!
## Other Identities
+ $$\overline{e^{i\theta}}=e^{-i\theta}$$
+ $$\arg(\overline{e^{i\theta}})=\arg(e^{-i\theta})=-\theta$$
## Applications
1) ==Save space!== We can write $e^{i\theta}$ instead of $\cos \theta+i\sin \theta$
	1) [[Polar Representation]] is now just $$z=re^{i\theta}, r>0$$
	2) Multiplication of complex numbers becomes simpler: $$\text{If }z=re^{i\theta},w=\rho e^{i\chi}\text{ then }zw=r \rho e^{i\theta}e^{i\chi}\implies\underline{zw=r \rho e^{i(\theta+\chi)}}$$
