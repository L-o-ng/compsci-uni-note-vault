#notes 

Recall that the gradient gives the best [[Linear Approximations|linear approximation]] to $f$: $$f(x_{0}+x)\approx f(x_{0})+f^\prime(x_{0})x$$
The **curvature** gives the best $2^{\text{nd}}$ order, or **quadratic** approximation: $$f(x_{0}+x) \approx f(x_{0})+f^\prime(x_{0})x+\frac{f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x_{0})}{2}x^2$$
At a stationary point, the second term is 0, so: $$f(x_{0}+x) \approx f(x_{0})+\frac{f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x_{0})}{2}x^2$$
This is a parabola open up when $f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x_{0})>0$ and parabola open down when $f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x_{0})<0$, indeterminate if $f{^\prime}{^\prime}(x_{0})=0$.
