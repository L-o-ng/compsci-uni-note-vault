#notes 

Let $A$ be an invertible $n\times n$ matrix.
Considering vectors in $\Bbb{R}^{n}$ as column vectors, we define:
$$
\array{\langle\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\rangle=A\mathbf{u} \cdot A\mathbf{v} \\ \text{or }\langle\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\rangle=(A\mathbf{v})^{T}A\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{v}^{T}A^{T}A\mathbf{u}}
$$
where the RHS uses the standard dot product in $\Bbb{R}^{n}$.
This is an inner product, called the inner product on $\Bbb{R}^{n}$ generated by $A$.
## Examples
1) The dot product on $\Bbb{R}^{n}$ is the inner product generated by the identity matrix.
2) The [[Weighted Euclidean Inner Product]] on $\Bbb{R}^{n}$ with weights $w_{1},\dots,w_{n}$ is the inner product generated by $A=\operatorname{diag}(\sqrt{ w_{1} },\dots,\sqrt{ w_{n} })$. For the example seen in [[Weighted Euclidean Inner Product]]:$$\langle\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\rangle=3u_{1}v_{1}+2u_{2}v_{2}=\begin{pmatrix}\sqrt{ 3 } & 0 \\ 0 & \sqrt{ 2 }\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}u_{1} \\ u_{2}\end{pmatrix}\cdot \begin{pmatrix}\sqrt{ 3 } & 0 \\ 0 & \sqrt{ 2 }\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}v_{1} \\ v_{2}\end{pmatrix}$$
