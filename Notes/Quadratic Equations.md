Solve $ax^2+bx+x=0$.
1) $\times4a$
2) $\implies4a^2x^2+4abx=-4ac$
3) $\implies(2ax+b)^2-b^2=4ac$
4) $\implies(2ax+b)^2=b^2-4ac$
Now there are multiple cases:
1) $b^2-4ac\lt0:\text{ No real solutions.}$
2) $b^2-4ac=0\implies2ax+b=0\implies x=-\frac{b}{2a}$
3) $b^2-4ac\gt0\implies2ax+b=\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}\implies x=\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$
Note the equation $y^2=x$ has 2 solutions when $x\gt0$: $y=\pm\sqrt{x}$
